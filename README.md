## 🌌 Core Technology Tracks

### 1. Freestanding Graphene Fluctuation Harvesters (Ambient Brownian Kinetic DC)
This track simulates the conversion of ambient, room-temperature thermal energy into micro-scale electrical power without requiring a temperature gradient ($\Delta T = 0$). 

* **The Physics:** Single-atom-thick freestanding graphene membranes exhibit continuous, intrinsic, out-of-plane fluctuations (atomic rippling and buckling) driven purely by room-temperature **Brownian motion**. 
* **The Transduction:** When suspended adjacent to an asymmetric metal sub-micron electrode, the shifting curvature of the graphene sheet modulates local capacitance, generating a low-frequency displacement current. This alternating current (AC) is routed through a specialized multi-diode nonlinear circuit layout that leverages stochastic thermodynamics to rectify and amplify the output into continuous direct current (DC).

### 2. All-Carbon Near-Field Thermophotovoltaic (TPV) Cells
This track models solid-state thermal energy storage and instantaneous high-efficiency exergy extraction.

* **The Storage:** A highly insulated block of high-density synthetic graphite stores thermal energy up to $1,500^\circ\text{C}$ (charged via stranded, overproduced solar or industrial waste streams).
* **The Quantum Tunneling:** Placed at sub-micron distances (sub-wavelength gaps), the system bypasses standard blackbody radiation limitations (Planck's Law) through **evanescent photon tunneling**. An aligned carbon nanotube (CNT) metamaterial emitter shapes the thermal spectrum, ensuring emitted photons match the exact bandgap of a receiving photovoltaic cell, extracting electricity silently with zero moving parts.
