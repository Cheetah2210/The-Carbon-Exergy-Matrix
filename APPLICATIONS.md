## 🛠️ Engineering Execution & Scaling Frameworks

### Track 1: Ambient Mesh Networks ($\Delta T = 0$)
* **Objective:** Power decentralized agricultural sensors, remote weather trackers, and municipal monitoring grids indefinitely without battery replacements.
* **Implementation:** Arrays of macro-scale chips containing millions of parallelized freestanding graphene cavities. The collective micro-ampere displacement currents simulated in `graphene_brownian.py` are routed through an onboard Schottky diode rectification matrix to trickle-charge a solid-state supercapacitor.

### Track 2: Industrial Exhaust Processing ($400^\circ\text{C} - 1200^\circ\text{C}$)
* **Objective:** Reclaim massive exergy losses from cement factories, steel manufacturing plants, and glass-blowing kilns.
* **Implementation:** Coaxial inline energy sleeves lined with synthetic graphite blocks are wrapped directly around high-temperature exhaust stacks. The interior surface features an aligned carbon nanotube (CNT) metamaterial emitter array. By maintaining a micro-vacuum gap between the hot flue sleeve and water-cooled static PV receptors, energy is extracted via the evanescent coupling mechanics mapped out in `near_field_tunneling.py`.

### Track 3: High-Fouling Geothermal Extraction
* **Objective:** Extract power from mineral-heavy, highly corrosive geothermal brine streams that rapidly destroy traditional mechanical turbine blades.
* **Implementation:** Raw geothermal fluid passes through open, non-obstructed channels lined with ultra-smooth, chemically inert Diamond-Like Carbon (DLC) coatings. Because the system is 100% solid-state with zero rotating blades, the mineral-heavy fluid transfers its thermal energy through the carbon shell to deep-set TPV conversion cells without any risk of mechanical binding, calcification lockup, or particulate fouling.

## 🚀 Repository Integration Notice

When adapting the simulation files (`variables/`) to model these global configurations, developers must adjust the initial boundary conditions within the physics scripts to reflect the target installation profile:

1. **For Industrial Tracking:** Scale up `T_emitter` inside `near_field_tunneling.py` to match the target chimney flue gas profile ($>1000\text{K}$).
2. **For Ambient Tracking:** Scale up the `electrode_area` and array-multiplier metrics within `graphene_brownian.py` to model macro-scale multi-cell harvest clusters.
